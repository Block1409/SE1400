Baccarat

Baccarat is a simple yet elegant casino card game played between two hands known as the “Player” and the “Banker,” with the goal of betting on which hand will finish closest to nine. The game is typically played with multiple standard 52-card decks, and each round begins with two cards dealt to both the Player and the Banker. Card values are added together, where tens and face cards count as zero, aces count as one, and only the last digit of the total is used (for example, 15 becomes 5). If either hand totals 8 or 9 on the initial deal, it is called a “natural,” and the round usually ends immediately; otherwise, a third card may be drawn according to fixed rules that determine when the Player or Banker must take an additional card, meaning there are no player decisions during gameplay. Before cards are dealt, players place bets on the Player hand, Banker hand, or a Tie, each with different payouts and odds. The Banker bet has a slightly higher chance of winning due to the drawing rules, though casinos usually take a small commission on Banker wins, while Tie bets pay more but are much less likely to occur. Because players have no control over actions after betting, baccarat is considered a pure game of chance driven entirely by probability. Despite its simplicity, it remains one of the most popular casino games in the world, especially in high-stakes settings, thanks to its fast pace, minimal rules, and consistent suspense with each round.
