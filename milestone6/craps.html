Craps

Craps is a fast-paced dice game commonly played in casinos and known for its energetic atmosphere and variety of betting options. The game centers around the roll of two six-sided dice, with players betting on the outcome of each roll or series of rolls. The most important roll is the “come-out roll,” which establishes the game’s initial result: a 7 or 11 results in an immediate win for pass line bets, while a 2, 3, or 12 results in a loss. Any other number becomes the “point,” and the shooter continues rolling the dice, aiming to roll the point number again before rolling a 7. If the point is rolled again first, pass line bets win; if a 7 appears first, they lose, a result known as “seven out.” Players can place a wide variety of additional bets, including come, don’t come, place bets, and proposition bets, each with different odds and payouts. While some bets have a higher house edge, others offer more favorable odds and are based more directly on the dice probabilities. Craps is largely a game of chance, but understanding the different betting options can influence strategy and risk. Its combination of simple core mechanics and complex betting structure makes craps one of the most exciting and social casino games, often drawing crowds around the table with every roll of the dice.
